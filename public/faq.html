What is Polandball?

"Polandball", or sometimes (but less frequently) refereed to as "Countryball" are "meme" comics that are satirically personified as country(ies) with it's history, policies as a nation, and foreign relations to other countries.

Where can I find out more information about these comics?

You can visit the wiki page below to get a general sense as to what and how to make a Polandball Comic, or visit the either the official website or Subreddit for Polandball comics (also below). Comics are posted daily (weekly on the official site) and are great places for inspiration.

Are there any rules to Polandball?

Yes there are quite a few. Most rules can be found on the wiki page but here are the brief rules on these comics:
1. The Countryballs must all be hand drawn using a paint program (i.e. Microsoft Paint). Tools on these programs such as the straight line/circle or fill tools are forbidden to draw these comics, only free form line paint tools may be used to draw these comics. Flags must also be hand drawn (no matter how good or bad that flag is drawn
2. Countryballs must be drawn as a circle/sphere with a black border around them. There are a few notable exceptions for certain countries (i.e. Israel Cube, Reichtangle, Nepalrawr etc.) in terms of what shape they must be drawn in.
3. Poland's flag must be drawn upside down in order to show eye contrast. Asian countries (i.e. China, Vietnam, Japan) usually are drawn with straight lines as eyes.
4. Countryballs CANNOT have limbs, pupils with their eyes, mouths, eyebrows or hair of any kind. Their emotions are typically displayed with their eye shape.
5. Certain Countryballs must contain clothes with their character (i.e. United Kingdom almost always has a hat and monocle, United States is drawn with sunglasses, Indonesia is drawn with a kopiah/rice hat in order to distinguish it from Poland, etc.)
6. All Countryballs' dialogue must be written in broken English tailored to their respective location (unless that said country speaks English as a primary language like The US) (note that just because a Countryball speaks in broken English, it does not mean the country's people speak the same way. The broken English is meant to be satirical and should not be taken seriously).
7. Any other memes, either displayed with dialogue and/or images (i.e. troll faces, "mlg banter", dank memes, etc.) are not allowed as they do not fit in with the comic's style.
8. Font styles that are hard to read or are Comics Sans are not allowed.

I'm glad you asked! This site allows you to make your own Polandball comics on your own without the hassle and/or difficulty of using paint programs! It allows you to express current news or historic topics that you are interested in through Polandball comic form if you don't have the skill or patience to make your own.

These are the rules that the community decided on. How do I correctly word the dialogue in my comic?

The best way to understand the "engrish" that this comic uses it to look up other comics that uses the countryball that you want to use (or is geographically/culturally similar) in order to correctly word your countryball's dialouge.

I don't have any ideas for my comic. Any advice?

I definitely recommend checking out current world news or read/watch some topics on history. There is a never ending stream of world news and there really isn't anything that you can't create, as long as it's related to a country's actions/thoughts/interests.

These comics are offensive.

The way these comics are made are meant to make fun of the government and not so much it's people. Either way these comics are not meant to be taken seriously. If you still find them offensive and want to voice your opinion, then unfortunately your arguments that you would make will probably go unnoticed or will get criticized. It's best to ignore, avoid, or just accept anything that you don't enjoy, because your opinion is most likely not going to change or remove this sub genre of comics.

How do I represent indigenous/native persons?

Indigenous are represented with billiard balls because most do not have a formal flag or nation. Indigenous Africans are represented with an 8 Ball, 7 Ball for Native Americans and Oceanic Tribes, 1 Ball for East Asians (including Mongolian tribes) and extra terrestrial life/aliens as 6 Balls.

I noticed that other Polandball Comics have props/objects in them. How do I put those in my comics?

You can simply do that by using the "Add Outside Object" button. You will need to draw the object that you want, unfortunately (there are just too many to draw). Make sure that you know where you saved your file, and use as little surrounding white space around your object, as that white space might overlap with another countryball/other object.
